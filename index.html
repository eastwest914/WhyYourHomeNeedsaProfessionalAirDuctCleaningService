FAQs: Common Air Duct & Dryer Vent Cleaning Questions

1. What is air duct cleaning?

Air duct cleaning is the process of removing dust, debris, mold, and other contaminants from your HVAC system's ductwork. It improves indoor air quality, enhances system efficiency, and reduces allergens and pollutants circulating through your home or business.

2. How long does air duct cleaning take?

Most residential air duct cleaning jobs take between 2 to 4 hours depending on the size of your system and the level of buildup. Commercial projects may take longer based on system complexity.

3. Do I need professional dryer vent cleaning?

Yes — clogged dryer vents are a leading cause of house fires. Professional dryer vent cleaning removes lint buildup that restricts airflow, reduces fire risk, improves dryer efficiency, and extends the life of your appliance.

4. How often should I have my air ducts cleaned?

Most experts recommend air duct cleaning every 3 to 5 years. However, if you have pets, allergies, recent renovations, or visible mold growth, more frequent cleaning may be beneficial.

5. Can air duct cleaning help with allergies?

Yes — removing dust, mold spores, pet dander, and other allergens from your ductwork can significantly improve indoor air quality and reduce allergy and asthma symptoms for occupants.
